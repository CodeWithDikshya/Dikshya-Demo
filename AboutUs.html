Common Eye Health Issues in Nepal

Allergic Eye Disease

Common in dusty areas, it causes redness, itching, and swelling of the eyes.

Cataracts

A major cause of blindness in Nepal. Often caused by aging or sun exposure.

Glaucoma

Progressive damage to the optic nerve—early detection is crucial to prevent blindness.

Refractive Errors

Uncorrected myopia, hyperopia, and astigmatism affect children and adults alike.

Diabetic Retinopathy

A serious complication of diabetes, affecting vision and potentially causing blindness.

Conjunctivitis

Highly contagious eye infection causing redness and watery discharge.

Reversible Diabetic Vision Loss

With early care, vision loss due to diabetes can be managed or reversed.

👦👧 Children's Eye Care Awareness 👁️

Protecting Little Eyes

  • 📚 Take breaks from reading and screen time—follow the 20-20-20 rule.
  • 🕶️ Wear sunglasses during outdoor play to protect from UV rays.
  • 🍎 Eat eye-healthy foods like carrots, leafy greens, and fruits.
  • 📵 Reduce phone and tablet time—balance screen with playtime.
  • 💧 Avoid rubbing eyes with dirty hands to prevent infection.
  • 🩺 Schedule regular eye checkups for early problem detection.

Healthy Habits for Kids

  • 🏃‍♀️ Encourage outdoor play—physical activity promotes eye health.
  • 🛏️ Ensure good sleep—proper rest is essential for vision development.
  • 🧴 Use protective eyewear for sports to prevent accidents.
  • 🥦 Include foods like fish, nuts, and eggs in the diet for strong vision.
  • 👀 Monitor any signs of eye strain or discomfort—early treatment is key.
  • 👓 Keep track of children’s vision changes—get regular eye exams.

Fun Eye Health Tips for Children

  • 🎮 Limit screen time to less than two hours a day.
  • 🌞 Keep eyes protected with a hat and sunglasses during outdoor play.
  • 👀 Make eye exercises fun to help them strengthen their vision.
  • 📏 Keep reading materials at a proper distance from eyes to avoid strain.
  • 🎉 Celebrate good vision health—make it a fun family activity!
